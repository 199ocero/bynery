## 5 Best Expense Tracker Apps of 2026

### 1. EasyReceipt — Best for Receipt-Based Expense Tracking

EasyReceipt turns receipt scanning into a complete expense tracking workflow. Scan a receipt, let the AI extract every field, then export a clean spreadsheet at the end of the month — no manual data entry at any step.

**Key features:**
- AI Smart Scan: automated extraction of merchant, date, items, and totals
- Multi-currency support with real-time exchange rate conversion
- Export to CSV and Google Sheets
- Expense summary dashboard (Power plan includes full export)
- Receipt history from 3 months (free) up to forever (Power)

**Pricing:**
- **Free** — $0/month: 10 scans/month, 3-month history
- **Pro** — $5.99/month: 200 scans/month, 1-year history, full multi-currency
- **Power** — $9.99/month: unlimited scans, forever history, expense summary export

**Best for:** Freelancers, small business owners, and travelers who want receipt-level detail in their expense records.

### 2. YNAB (You Need a Budget) — Best for Zero-Based Budgeting

YNAB is the gold standard for people who want to plan every dollar before they spend it. It does not scan receipts but connects to bank accounts and helps you allocate money across budget categories proactively.

**Key features:**
- Zero-based budgeting methodology
- Bank and credit card sync
- Goal tracking and debt payoff tools
- Strong educational resources

**Limitation:** $14.99/month — the most expensive app on this list. No receipt scanning or multi-currency support.

### 3. Mint — Best Free Bank-Sync Option

Mint automatically pulls transactions from your bank and credit card accounts, categorizes them, and shows you a spending overview. No manual input needed for card purchases — but cash and receipt tracking is non-existent.

**Key features:**
- Free with bank account sync
- Automatic transaction categorization
- Bill tracking and credit score monitoring
- Spending trends dashboard

**Limitation:** No receipt scanning; cash expenses are invisible unless entered manually.

### 4. Expensify — Best for Team Expense Reports

Expensify is built for businesses that need approval workflows — employees submit receipts, managers approve, finance exports to accounting software. Its SmartScan captures receipt totals but misses line-item detail.

**Key features:**
- SmartScan OCR for receipt totals
- Multi-currency support
- Approval workflows and policy enforcement
- Integrations with QuickBooks, Xero, NetSuite

**Limitation:** Per-seat pricing gets expensive; no Google Sheets export.

### 5. Notion — Best DIY Expense Tracker

Notion is not an expense app but many freelancers and small teams use it as one — building custom databases that connect to Google Sheets, log expenses by project, and generate monthly summaries.

**Key features:**
- Fully customizable expense database
- Google Sheets integration via third-party tools
- Free tier is generous
- Works for both personal and team tracking

**Limitation:** Requires setup time; no native receipt scanning or bank sync.
