## The Digital Nomad Expense Tracking Problem

Most nomads underestimate how complicated their finances get within the first year of full-time travel. Common problems:

- **Multiple tax residencies** — many countries tax you if you stay more than 183 days; clean expense records help establish where you actually lived and worked
- **Cash-heavy economies** — Southeast Asia, parts of Latin America, and Eastern Europe still run heavily on cash; card statements miss these entirely
- **Currency fluctuation** — an expense recorded at the wrong exchange rate can create a meaningful discrepancy at tax time
- **Receipt decay** — thermal paper receipts fade fast in heat and humidity; scan them the same day

AI receipt scanning with automatic currency conversion solves problems 2, 3, and 4 simultaneously.

## How Digital Nomads Should Organize Annual Expenses for Tax Filing

Most digital nomads file taxes in their home country, a country they established tax residency in, or both. Regardless of jurisdiction, accountants need the same basic data:

1. **Total income** by client and currency
2. **Total expenses** by category and currency
3. **Converted amounts** in your tax home currency on the date of the transaction
4. **Supporting documentation** — receipts for anything above the minimum threshold

EasyReceipt's Google Sheets export gives you points 2, 3, and 4 automatically. Combine it with your invoicing records and bank statements for a complete annual financial picture.
