## The Freelance Expense Tracking Workflow That Saves Hours at Tax Time

The freelancers who spend the least time on tax prep follow a simple monthly routine:

1. **Scan every receipt immediately** — use EasyReceipt right after a purchase while the receipt is still clean and flat
2. **Export to Google Sheets monthly** — run a monthly export and drop it into your expense tracking template
3. **Flag deductible categories** — meals, software, travel, home office, and professional development are the most common freelance deductions
4. **Send the spreadsheet to your accountant** — a clean CSV with line-item detail saves significant billable accountant hours

This workflow takes about 15 minutes per month if you scan receipts consistently. It takes hours if you do it all at once in April.

## Freelance Tax Deductions You Should Be Tracking in 2026

According to IRS guidelines, common deductible freelance expenses include:

- **Home office** — proportional rent or mortgage interest if you have a dedicated workspace
- **Software and subscriptions** — tools you use exclusively for work
- **Client meals** — 50% deductible; receipt detail matters here
- **Travel** — flights, hotels, and ground transport for client work
- **Professional development** — courses, books, and conferences
- **Equipment** — laptops, phones, cameras used for work

Line-item receipt data from EasyReceipt makes documenting each of these deduction categories significantly easier.
